Without a doubt, I have been passionate about many things within my lifetime. As expected, some of these passions were short and some were long. Here, I'll be talking about four of my biggest passions that are still prevelent to this day. Let's get started!!

Art

For me, art has always been something that has helped me stay grounded, whether it be traditional or digital art.

Favourite Memory

For over fifty hours, I drew one of my favourite online celebrities - Janet. Although there are many occurences that come to mind when it comes to art, the amount of work and effort placed into this was unlike any other. Prior to this, I have never done a large scale portrait so upon completion, I felt a sense of pride and wonder that I was able to complete it.

Lessons:


- You never know when opportunities might come knocking on your door so it’s important to be prepared for when it does come around.
- Everyone is on their own journey at their own pace. Some people may have started earlier but that doesn’t mean you won’t get their yourself.

Athletics

At the age of five, I began learning piano. Honestly, I can’t say I remember much other than the fact that there were many times when I didn’t feel like practicing, annoyed by how slowly I was improving and how repetitive practicing was. I would complain about this and my parents would ask me if I wanted to quit. Every time, this question would immediately quiet me because I refused to not be able to reach a certain speed or evoke a certain emotion or achieve a certain sense of clarity or so on. Before I knew it, I found myself looking forward to the challenge of being able to overcome these struggles. I started setting more goals and I became more passionate about music. I began to try more instruments on my own time, in school and in cadet band such as the violin, flute, alto saxophone and ukulele.

Favourite Memory

At the Tom Lee Centre in Vancouver of 2014??, I played my heart out in front of two examiners in a one-hour performance. Despite my stage fright, I enjoyed the feeling of being able to demonstrate all the hard work I have put into practicing within the ten years prior. Through all the tears and smiles, and the failures and successes, I was able to come out and put on a show; thus, earning my Associate Diploma of Music in Piano Performance.

Lessons:


- Things don’t just show up within a day (no matter how much we may wish it). Hard work and time creates results.
- Being able to prioritize and managing time will improve your quality of life greatly.
- There are ups and downs in life but you WILL get through.

Gaming

I’ve been playing video games for as long as I can remember. From faithfully feeding my Tamagotchi four times a day to playing League of Legends, I’ve always loved being able to immerse myself in a different world. Along the way, I began to ask questions. How was it made? How was it being run? As I grew older, the characters, stories and dynamics of computer games, and the conversations with engineering professionals sparked my fascination and grew into a desire to experiment with coding on my own and __.

Favourite Memory

One of my favourite memories is actually how I started playing League of Legends. I haven't actually started paying PC games yet. Probably an unorthodox scenario, I happened to bring my laptop over to a sleepover. My friend, an avid gamer, decided to download League on my laptop and before I knew it, I was trying out something new! Who knew something like that could lead to a passion and eventually influence my career choices?

Lessons:


- You can never be certain of an outcome. Even if it seems like you are losing, it doesn’t mean that you can’t have a perfect next fight that can change the entire game (and vice versa).
- Communication and trust will make or break a team. Even with the best individual players, without these foundations, the team will not be the best.
- It’s important to just have fun and rewind because balance is extremely important!
